Yearly average rainfall across Australia from 2008 to 2017.

  • Wettest period: 2010–2011 sits clearly above the mean, indicating an unusually wet couple of years.

Top 10 Locations (by Year)

Top 10 locations with the highest average rainfall each year.

  • Cairns & Coffs Harbour lead often; Darwin surges to #1 in 2017 with the decade’s peak.
  • Tropical/east-coast hubs dominate (Cairns, Darwin, Townsville, Coffs Harbour, Gold Coast, Sydney/Wollongong/Newcastle); inland/southern sites are rare.
  • Year-to-year swings: strong around 2010–11, softer 2014–16, then a sharp rebound in 2017.

Rainfall by State

Rainfall distribution across states for the selected year.

  • Top-end stays wettest: Northern Territory and Queensland dominate state averages most years, with standout peaks in QLD ~5.74 mm (2010) and NT ~5.70 mm (2017).
  • South/temperate states are driest: SA, TAS, VIC and ACT mostly sit around ~1–2 mm; lows include SA ~0.98 mm (2015) and ACT ~1.05 mm (2017).
  • Clear year-to-year swings: Broad spike 2010–11 (especially QLD/NT), a trough around 2014–15, then a rebound by 2016–17.

Rainfall vs Temperature

How rainfall levels vary with temperature across seasons.

  • Winter and Spring are the wettest seasons: The higher concentration of blue (Winter) and green (Spring) dots shows most rainfall occurs during cooler months with moderate temperatures (10°C–25°C).
  • Rainfall decreases as temperature rises: The downward trend suggests an inverse relationship; cooler months tend to be wetter, while hotter months receive less rainfall.
  • Summer and Autumn have rare extremes: A few red (Summer) and yellow (Autumn) outliers show heavy rain with high temperatures, likely from tropical regions like Darwin and Cairns.

Seasonal Rainfall Patterns

Average rainfall trends across months, highlighting seasonal patterns.

  • Wettest months (Dec–Mar): These summer months show the highest average rainfall, reflecting Australia’s northern wet season influenced by tropical monsoons.
  • Driest period (Jun–Oct): Mid to late year experiences the lowest rainfall, representing the national dry season.
  • Clear seasonal pattern: The smooth rise and fall of the curve reveal a predictable annual rainfall cycle with strong contrasts between wet and dry periods.

Summary: Australia’s rainfall seasonality reflects two overlapping systems: tropical summer monsoons in the north and winter westerly rain in the south.
